# Beginner Variant - Messaging Strategy

**Date:** 2026-02-18
**Variant ID:** `beginner-motion-vibe`
**Traffic Sources:** YouTube, Twitter (X), Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn (creative professionals)

---

## ## Who This Person Is

- Uses AI for static images (Midjourney, Flux) and text (ChatGPT, Claude) regularly.
- Wants to create motion graphics or explainer videos but finds After Effects or Blender too steep a learning curve.
- Has heard of "vibe coding" (using AI to write code) but thinks it's only for building apps or websites.
- Views themselves as a **"Creative"** or **"Storyteller,"** not a "Coder" or "Animator."
- Primary emotion: **Frustrated ambition** (has the vision, lacks the technical hands).

## ## Who This Person Is NOT

- They aren't professional motion designers looking to optimize a complex workflow.
- They aren't trying to become full-stack software engineers.
- They haven't successfully used Python or Manim before (they might have tried and quit).
- They don't care about clean code—they care about the **final video output**.
- They aren't looking for "one-click" generic AI video generators; they want *control* over the details, which is why they are interested in the coding aspect (precision).

---

## ## The Core Narrative Arc

The entire page follows one emotional journey:

### ### 1. Recognition (Hero)
"You have a clear vision for an explainer video in your head, but your hands can't execute it fast enough."

### ### 2. Identification (Problem)
"You've tried traditional animation software, but it's a maze of keyframes and graph editors. Or you've tried generic AI video generators, but they lack the precision you need. You feel stuck between 'too hard' and 'too random.'"

### ### 3. Hope (Solution)
"There is a third way. It's called Vibe Coding. It turns your natural language descriptions into precise animation code, giving you the control of a pro animator with the ease of a chat interface."

### ### 4. Belief (Case Study + Testimonials)
"Creators with zero coding background are generating broadcast-quality motion graphics just by describing the 'vibe' and movement they want. If you can describe it, you can render it."

### ### 5. Understanding (About + Curriculum)
"We strip away the syntax and focus on the logic of motion. Learn how to prompt for physics, timing, and transitions using AI coding tools."

### ### 6. Decision (Pricing + Final CTA)
"Stop fighting with keyframes. Start speaking your animations into existence. Join the new wave of motion creators."

---

## ## Key Messaging Rules

### ### Never Assume
- **Don't assume** they know what libraries like Manim, p5.js, or Three.js are.
- **Don't assume** they enjoy the process of debugging code.
- **Don't assume** they want to be called "developers."
- **Don't assume** they are satisfied with the "wobbly" quality of generative video (Sora/Runway)—highlight that code-based motion is *crisp* and *exact*.
- **Don't assume** they know how to set up a local development environment (focus on browser-based or simple setups).
