## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience: startup founders/CTOs evaluating a technical build partner, SME/agency clients seeking web design and digital strategy, and recruiters/employers considering Felix for a role or contract. All share one thing: they arrive via referral, portfolio, or search — and are actively deciding whether to reach out. The page must earn trust and trigger that contact.

### Primary Goal
Drive contact/hire inquiries. Every section should funnel toward a conversation — not just inform, but compel action.

### Brand Personality
**Bold · Unconventional · Technical.** Felix is a creative technologist who ships. The site should feel like a person made it — opinionated, crafted, surprising. Not a marketing department, not a template. The work is the proof; the site is the pitch.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark navy (`#011627`) + lime green primary — a deliberate, committed palette, not the default "dark mode with cyan accents" AI palette
- Unbounded (display) + Poppins (body) + DM Mono (footer) — strong typographic hierarchy
- Motion-forward: GSAP scroll reveals, pixelated video hero, dot matrix overlays, physics tags — animation is a feature, not decoration
- Anti-references: no hero→services→testimonials→CTA cookie-cutter structure, no safe/corporate vibes, no glassmorphism as decoration

### Design Principles
1. **The work is the argument** — portfolio imagery and case studies carry more persuasive weight than copy; give them space
2. **Motion must mean something** — every animation should communicate state or reveal information, not just add flair
3. **Contact is the exit** — every section implicitly points toward a conversation; the CTA is never far away
4. **Personality over polish** — rough edges and strong opinions beat sterile perfection
5. **Typography as identity** — Unbounded in massive italic-black is the brand voice; use it with confidence
